Sprint

This game was created using Unity C#, and the goal of the game is to reach the end of each level as fast as you can, setting / beating your own personal bests, it was developed for my Final Year Project module at University, and was also polished up and shown at "Expo Tees". It was inspired by games like Clustertruck, SEUM: Speedrunners from hell, Karlson, and Speedrunners.

In the University module, I created a proposal that outlined what I had planned the project to be, my motivations behind it, my development plan, and any research I possibly needed to do. Then came the development phase, during which I designed, and developed the game. I also updated my tutor with a progress report presentation, that went through what I'd done for the first half of the module. Once development was over, I then had to create a video showcasing everything in the final product, and a report that went into detail on everything I had done, including an evaluation on my development process.

Samsara Spell: Painful Downfall

This spell is an AOE for "Samsara", a game by INFINITY 27. I designed and developed it in Unreal Engine 4, using blueprints, during a 2-week skills bootcamp. The spell spreads 3 points of damage per-second across all enemies within the range, doing a minimum of 1 point of damage. It will follow the targeted enemy, or the player if an enemy casts the spell, players can also dodge away from the spell to stop it following them. Spells in this game are affected by something called "Wisdom points", with my spell, it can increase the AOE size by a maximum of 70%, and the damage by a maximum of 10 points.

I signed up to the 2-week skills bootcamp at INFINITY 27 to learn more about how a live game studio works. During my time there, I learned more about Agile & Scrum methodologies, and carried out daily stand-up meetings to update the team (and other applicants) on my proggress, and ask for help where needed. At the end of the bootcamp, I was given feedback from my tutor that said I showed good communication, understood and carried out coding & naming practices decently, and documented my work well.

Gravity Switch

This project was originally one of my first Unreal Engine projects, unfortunately lost to drive corruption, but recently I brought the concept back and developed it using Unity, in the Industry Portfolio Development module at University, the main goal of this module was to teach us how to create relevent documents for applying to jobs in the industry, including creating a project to show on our portfolios.

The original concept of this project was for a portal-inspired puzzle game, that instead played with gravity as it's main puzzle mechanic. rather than portals. This project was later polished up and turned into a tech demo to show off the mechanics developed for it, as I wasn't confident in my Level Design skills enough to continue developing it into a full game.

Experimental Flight Movement

This is a personal project of mine, created in my spare time using Unity C#. The goal of this project was to explore a new type of movement that was less common in games, that being superhero flight. In this demo the player can smoothly switch between running on the ground, to flying or going super-sonic in-air. I don't plan on continuing this into a complete game or demo just yet.
